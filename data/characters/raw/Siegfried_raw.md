**Siegfried** (Hangul: 지크프리트, *Ji-keu-peu-ri-teu*) is a high grade Fixer employed by K Corp. and an old friend of Vergilius. He is a supporting character in Canto III: The Unconfronting.

## Appearance
Siegfried is a tall, muscular man with white eyes and shoulder-length blonde hair with two plaits. His most notable feature are his prosthetic arms made of an engraved wood or brass-like material. The right arm ends in a massive fist whereas the left possess a normal-sized silver hand capable of firing energy blasts; both are attached to prosthetic shoulders with two thin joints and three cables. Siegfried wears a blue bodysuit with green details on the torso, lower legs and neck and a green domino mask with a yellow border. Additionally, he wears a large black fur coat (with two massive clawed paws still attached) fastened at the neck by a silver brooch with eight small fastenings on the left.
## Personality
Siegfried is energetic and enthusiastic with a flair for showmanship. He enjoys attention, attending magazine interviews, and encouraging witnesses to spread stories of him. He even encourages the Sinners to take photographs of him before he defeats them, despite photos normally requiring permission from K Corp. Siegfried appears to hold no grudges against his opponents, allowing Don Quixote (whom he had just killed) to acquire an autograph. He is rather flippant, teasing Sinners while fighting them and complimenting Vergilius for his sharp tongue after being insulted.
Siegfried tends to speak in an archaic and formal fashion and refers to his actions has "heroism".
## Story
After the Sinners' violation of the K Corp. checkpoint Taboos, Siegfried appeared before them to handle the commotion. After a brief conversation with Vergilius, he flaunted his overwhelming power and defeats every Sinner, excluding Dante, with one hand in 46.5 seconds, an action approved by Vergilius to teach the Sinners a lesson. He left shortly after giving Don Quixote his autograph, going off to attend a magazine interview.

## Trivia
Siegfried is inspired by Sigurd, also known as Siegfried, a legendary knight from Germanic mythology, prominently featured in the epic poem the Nibelungenlied*.
In the *Nibelungenlied*, Siegfried is a valiant knight who has become invulnerable after slaying a dragon and bathing in its blood, but he is eventually defeated by the warrior Hagen, who knew of a spot in Siegfried's body that hadn't been touched by the dragon's blood, and was still vulnerable.
Siegfried was first alluded to in a tweet by the Limbus Company Twitter account in 2021. The tweet features a conversation between Gebura and Roland who talk about the strongest Fixers in the City. Several names are mentioned, including Sieg (지크, *ji-keu*).
Hagen (Hangul: 하겐, *ha-gen*), the warrior who kills Siegfried in the *Nibelungenlied* is also mentioned, although he has not yet been referenced in *Limbus Company*.
Siegfried's autobiography is titled "A Hero Should Keep Smiling Until Their Last".[1]
