**Wolf** (Hangul: 늑대, *Neug-dae*) is a member of a mysterious group along with Lion and Panther that, among other things, aims to hunt down and kill Dante for unknown reasons. The trio appear to have knowledge of Dante's identity and purpose prior to the replacement of their head. She is an antagonist introduced in Prologue: Selva Oscura.

## Appearance
Wolf is a short woman with long and spiky blue hair, traveling down to her shoulders. Her bangs are brought down over her right eye, concealing it, while the left side of her head is shorter, gray, hair, which reveals her other eye. She wears a fur-lined black coat that goes down to her knees, with a buckle midway down her torso. On her left arm, there is a wrapped purple snake emblem. She has two gloved hands, and wears torn black pants and similarly dark shoes. She carries a large silver sword which has a pommel that sports a large tuft of blue hair, similar in color to her hair color.
## Personality
Wolf is a focused individual who values the lessons she learned from her mentor and does not like to waste time. She is less emotive than her partners, overall allowing them to speak and demonstrate their abilities.
## Story
Wolf, Lion, and Panther are met just as they corner Dante in a dark forest as a part of a plan made by their master. They decided to kill them once they realize that, thanks to Dante's new head, they will be unable to get any information out of them. While Lion wanted to draw it out, both Wolf and Panther agree it would be best to kill them quickly. Just as Wolf was about to kill Dante, they heard a noise, realizing there is a bus heading towards them. The Sinners exited the bus and engage the three in a fight, but Wolf and her companions made short work of them and are only stopped when Vergilius dismembers Wolf, taking a leg, and Lion, removing an arm, before he orders Panther to drag them away in a retreat.

## Trivia
Panther, Lion and Wolf are based on the three beasts from the first Canto of Dante's Inferno, which symbolize the three major divisions of the sins punished in hell. The she-wolf, the last beast encountered by Dante, is believed to represent the sins of incontinence, lack of self-restraint, and the deadly sin of greed, making the beast a representation of the sins of the city of Florence. As these sins are considered by Dante to be at the root of all political unrest in the land, the she-wolf is considered the most dangerous of the three beasts.
Though their master is currently unknown, Vergilius referring to them as "the Serpent", as well as the presence of purple snake ornaments on all three group members, it is possible the identity of the master is none other than Iori, the Purple Tear.
