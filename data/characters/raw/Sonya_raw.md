**Sonya** (Hangul: 소냐, *so-nya*) is the leader of the Yurodiviye, a Syndicate he co-founded with his long-term friend Rodion. Through him, the Yurodiviye has a business relationship with N Corp. and agreed to assist Hermann in her collecting of the Golden Boughs. He is a supporting character introduced in Canto II: The Unloving.

## Appearance
Sonya is a tall man of pale complexion. He has short, messy white hair that frames his face, with bangs swept over his forehead, and intense purple eyes. He wears layered clothing, consisting of a black vest and a grey tailcoat that reaches his calves, and white pants. He wears an ammunition belt around his right thigh, and a black belt around his waist and shoulder, from which hangs a long broadsword with a golden tassel. Around his neck hangs a pair of black-rimmed goggles with orange lenses, and he wears a black and white scarf around his neck and left arm, bearing the symbol of the Yurodiviye.
## Personality
Sonya is a calm, composed individual who speaks in a temperate and confident manner. He is a well-read and intelligent man, as well as a proficient spokesperson capable of rallying people under a just cause unfitting of the City's usual cruelty. Rodion often recalled him sitting at his desk, immersed in his studies. Additionally, he is noted to have spread the Yurodiviye throughout every Nest in the City, possessing a wide variety of connections.
Despite his reserved attitude, Sonya possesses a great passion: a dream of destroying the systems of the City which exploit the lower class, offering true liberation to the misfortunate by distributing wealth to all of those in need. In the pursuit of this dream, he is more than willing to accept costs and losses—including the deaths of his own men—seeing them as inevitable sacrifices in the makings of progress towards his goal. In contrast to Rodya, who grew hotheaded and desperate in the face of her people's suffering, Sonya is characterized as exceedingly patient, calculating, and future-oriented. While Rodya criticized him for planning endlessly without actually helping anyone in need, Hermann instead considered Sonya to be unlike most idealists who are all theory and no action.
Rodya was disgusted by Sonya's passivity, which he justifies as "waiting for the right moment" to change the world. However, Intervallo IV: Timekilling Time suggests that he has made impressive strides towards realizing the Yurodiviye's goal of wealth equality by staying true to these methods. Despite this, Timekilling Time also paints a somewhat questionable image of Sonya. The members of his Syndicate are shown to refer to him as "Saint" and look to him very highly, without any concern as to his intentions or methods, lending to a cult-like impression in to contrast to his stated dream for the City's people to be equal. His interactions with Rodion in Canto II also come off as somewhat haughty, acting as if he were superior to her. Here, Sonya is dismissive of Rodya's own accounts and feelings, overwriting them with his own unshakeable interpretation of the narrative. A petty and arrogant side to his personality is further established by his decision to bring up her lack of mark and therefore incompetence as soon as she started to get the best of him in their conversation, demonstrating that Sonya is ultimately far from the perfect figure he presents himself as.
## Story
### Prior to Limbus Company*
Coming from District 25, he was described as a country boy by Rodya and apparently never had a good relationship with money. Similarly, he tried to keep Rodya away from money and gambling, but ultimately failed.
Much of his life prior to founding Yurodiviye is unknown, but it's said that he had always been a dreamer, with the wish to abolish the system of oppression towards the poor and give everyone a right to psychological and intellectual delights regardless of class.
### Canto II: The Unloving
Sonya is one of the four Syndicate leaders gambling for the chance at taking control over the J Corp. casino and the Golden Bough found inside. He arrived late to the game and spent a moment catching up with Rodion about the status of the Yurodiviye before the game begins. The reason he wants the Golden Bough is the same reason he founded his Syndicate: to destroy the system of oppression and exploration caused by those who hoard the wealth.
During the final round of the game, Rodya made a suspicious move, causing the Tieqiu Boss to accuse her of cheating. Sonya asked to check her arm for a Wish sticker, but the boss of the Tieqiu Crew insisted on checking it himself, given the fact that Sonya and Rodion knew each other and therefore might be working together. It turned out that she did not use any Wishpower, asking what will happen to a player who accused another of cheating. Sonya answered with a demonstration, a trash compactor quickly crushing the Tieqiu boss flat. He pointed out that this is exactly what Rodya had wanted back when they were both members of the Yurodivye and asked if she would like to return to the Syndicate. Before she can answer, the Sinners crash through the door and interrupt the conversation. Sonya acknowledged these new 'friends' of hers, then declares that she won the game and that he has no objections to her victory before leaving the room.
At the bottom floor of Branch J-03 the Sinners reached a large castle made of ice, finding that Sonya has been waiting there for them to arrive. He explained to Rodion that before she got here there was no castle, simply walls of ice, and that she was currently resonating with the Golden Bough which caused these changes to happen. This castle is now a glimpse into Rodion's past, a confessional for the sin that has been haunting her for years. The two revisited her memories of the cruel pawnbroker who controlled their hometown, letting countless go hungry for the sake of her own greed. The Yurodiviye was doing nothing to stop her, nothing to help the starving children that Rodion wished to save when she co-founded it, so she had taken up the axe herself and killed the woman. While Rodion claimed she committed the murder for the sake of her neighbors, Sonya disagreed and believes that she only did it to feel special. He explains that the pawnbroker was the sister of a member of The Middle, and how, after only a short time of living free and eating well, the residents of the Backstreets were killed in mass as punishment. He offered Rodya a chance at using the Golden Bough's power to create a world free of starvation and struggle, but she turns him down. He accepted her choice the same way he accepted her win in the poker game and lets her retrieve the Golden Bough without a fight.
As the Sinners attempted to escape the Ice Castle Where Neighbors Lie after retrieving the Golden Bough, Sonya shows up once again, this time to provide a distraction for them. Rodion does not trust that his offer comes without ulterior motives, but, given the lack of other options, they accept his help. He tells Dante that "That Child" will be visiting them soon, and Sinclair sees something briefly appear on the man's forehead. Just before the Sinners run off to make their retreat, Sonya informed them that the next time they meet, he will likely not be as kind as he was today, as nothing will cause him to hesitate once he sees a real chance at bringing prosperity to the people.
After leaving Branch J-03, Sonya met with Hermann to discuss how the Golden Bough retrieval went. It had been Hermann who came up with the idea of showing Rodion an idealized world with the hope it would convince her to rejoin the Yurodiviye and, as a result, let the Golden Bough fall into their hands. He confessed that he is glad that the plan didn't work, because it meant that Rodya hasn't changed since he had last seen her, asking if she is going to scold him for failing the mission. Hermann simply explained that she is well aware that complaining will not change the past outcome and she trusts that she will come out on top at the end.
### Canto VIII: The Surrendered Witnessing
Now assisting the New League of Nine Littérateurs, Sonya reached Hongyuan following the end of the second round of the Daguanyuan Family Hierarch Evaluations as the various factions assemble to aid their preferred candidates. Working under a team of rookies led by Nelly, Sonya seemingly assisted in the extraction of Jia Huan and Gubo.

## Trivia
Sonya is based off the character of the same name from *Crime and Punishment* by Fyodor Dostoevsky.
In the novel, Sonya is a devout Christian woman from a poor family who works as a prostitute in order to support her family, and she is a friend of the sister of the pawnbroker Rodion had killed. Sonya is the first person Rodion confesses his crime to. Upon hearing of the murders he committed, she encourages him to confess to the police to help put an end to his internal torment.
Sonya is a bearer of The Sign. He is able to see the mark on some of the Sinners and Sinclair briefly witnesses the mark appear on his brow. He is also familiar with Demian, for reasons likely related to them both bearing the Sign, and was aware that he had plans to make contact with the Sinners soon after the events of Canto II.
