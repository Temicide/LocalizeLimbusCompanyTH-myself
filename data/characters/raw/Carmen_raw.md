**Carmen** (Hangul: 카르멘, *ka-reu-men*) was a scientist invested in Cogito research in the hope of curing humanity's "disease of the mind" who would go on to become a looming figure across the Project Moon universe. It was her strong grip on the hearts of the people around her which vastly influenced the plot and characters of both *Lobotomy Corporation* and *Library of Ruina*.
Following the events which established Lobotomy Corporation, Carmen took a place within the Light, and now acts as a mysterious voice guiding City residents into becoming Distortions.

## Appearance
Carmen is a woman sporting long and choppy brown hair tied back into a ponytail and adorned with a red bear hair clip. She is seen wearing an olive-green, collared dress with four buttons, and a black miniskirt underneath. Over her dress, she dons a white lab coat draping past her knees. Her shoes are the color of her dress, with the toe to the heel being a darker shade similar to her skirt.
## Personality
Carmen is a character most largely defined by others' impressions of her, rather than her own time in the spotlight. Lobotomy Corporation*– as told by Ayin, who cared dearly for her– paints an unabashedly positive and sympathetic image of Carmen. She is depicted as intensely dedicated to her work and the happiness of those around her, acting as a guiding source of hope amidst a long-doomed world. She was known for having a particular draw to her and a good eye for people. Ayin describes her as a noble woman who cared so deeply for others that she neglected caring for herself. Her extreme distress and eventual suicide upon Enoch's death and the scorn she received for it back these impressions up.
Contradicting these accounts, however, are occasionally very concerning and intimidating depictions of Carmen. Netzach tells "X" that a voice came to him saying he must ask their "permission for anything. Even for death". Looks into Chesed's former life show Carmen claiming that she already knew without doubt that he would join her project, and that "no matter how great [he] may be, [he] will always be in the palm of [her] hand". The immense influence Carmen held over others sometimes carried an unshakably eerie undertone. Roland would form an iffy impression of her even through the Sephirah's praise.[1]
The shapeless, charming voice that is Carmen throughout *Library of Ruina* and *Limbus Company* falls somewhere in between the more generous and questionable accounts of her. She states outright that she only wishes to help the people of the City to be true to themselves and love themselves for who they are. Yet her actions match her words poorly when her methods—causing Distortions—consistently lead her targets into amplified emotional suffering and an uncontrollable violence.
Carmen's true nature and personality ultimately come out to be as formless as her current state of self, with handfuls of different characters arguing in favor of vastly different impressions of her, and devastations caused with what she asserts are nothing but good intentions.

## Trivia
The red bear character on Carmen's hairclip is called "Kkomi" and can be seen worn by multiple background characters across certain *Limbus Company* CGs.
Carmen is the narrator of all Identity Stories that are not narrated by the Sinner themself.[2][3]
Carmen likes to claim that she's 170cm (5'7) tall.[4]
