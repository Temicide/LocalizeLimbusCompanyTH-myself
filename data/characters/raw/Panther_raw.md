**Panther** (Hangul: 표범, *Pyo-beom*) is a member of a mysterious group along with Lion and Wolf that, among other things, aims to hunt down and kill Dante. The trio appear to have knowledge of Dante's identity and purpose prior to the replacement of their head. He is an antagonist introduced in Prologue: Selva Oscura.

## Appearance
Panther is an average height, lean man with tanned skin and dark black hair cut just before his shoulders. Panther has dark grey, almost black eyes and sports black facial hair as well. He wears a single, purple earring on his left ear in the shape of a twisting snake. His outfit consists of a black hooded sheepskin cloak, held in the middle by a grey belt. The cloak has silver buckled pockets and grey wool trim on the hood, sleeves and in a thin stripe down the middle of the cloak. He wears black and grey buckled boots. The cloak is worn over a black turtleneck. Panther also wields a silver, double-sided halberd with gold edges on the blades and a purple tassel tied to the spear end of the weapon.
## Personality
Panther is a rational and observational person, being the one to focus the discussion on what information they could gather. He can also be cruel, as seen by his comment on wanting to see Dante's expression while they died and his remark on the Sinners being so weak that they are no fun to kill. Despite this, he is not overconfident, retreating when told to.
## Story
Panther, Lion, and Wolf are met just as they corner Dante in a dark forest as a part of a plan made by their master. They decided to kill them once they realize that, thanks to Dante's new head, they will be unable to get any information out of them. While Lion wanted to draw it out, both Panther and Wolf agree it would be best to kill them quickly. Just as Wolf was about to kill Dante, they heard a noise, realizing that a bus was heading towards them. The Sinners exited the bus and engage the three in a fight, but Panther and his companions made short work of them and are only stopped when Vergilius dismembers Wolf and Lion and orders Panther to drag them away in a retreat.

## Trivia
Panther, Lion and Wolf are based on the three beasts from the first Canto of Dante's Inferno, which symbolize the three major divisions of the sins punished in hell. The panther, the first beast encountered by Dante, is believed to represent fraud and malice, and particularly the deadly sins of envy and lust.
Though their master is currently unknown, Vergilius referring to them as "the Serpent", as well as the presence of purple snake ornaments on all three group members, it is possible the identity of the master is none other than Iori, the Purple Tear.
