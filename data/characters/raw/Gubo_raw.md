**Gubo** (Hangul: 구보, *gu-bo*) is an antagonist that first appeared as a minor antagonist in the webcomic, *Leviathan*, in Book7 - Ingress, where he infiltrated the Ring's corridor in hopes of finding his fellow member, Aseah. Notably, he takes the side of Hermann and her New League of Nine Littérateurs, looking to acquire the mysterious Golden Bough. He was first introduced as a minor antagonist in Canto I: The Outcast.

## Appearance
Gubo is a stern-looking man with short black hair and dark red eyes. He wears round glasses, at times with red-tinted lenses. He appears in the N Corp. uniform of Hermann's team, consisting of a dark blue long coat with red plaid lapels; his company ID is attached to his left lapel. Underneath the company uniform, he wears a grey waistcoat over a white-collered dress shirt with a red tie, and a pair of grey pants with shoes to match.
In combat, Gubo wields a curved karambit knife.
During Canto VIII: The Surrendered Witnessing, he dons the E.G.O Gear of Der Fluchschütze, a brown suit similar to his normal outfit, and a tattered cloak. Additionally, crimson flames flow upward from his back.
## Personality
Highly professional, Gubo is a man who acts through the path of least resistance, frequently choosing to avoid unnecessary conflict while still aiming for a positive benefit in his favor, such as his abiding of the Ring's customs in Leviathan*, aiming less to respect them and more to act for his own benefit. He carries an aura of unflappability, cold and calculating to the world around him. This, however, is recognized by Vergilius to be a façade; even when attempting to appear collected, he frequently is displeased and uncomfortable with the situations that go against his favor.
In the past, Gubo was a notable loner, failing to fraternize with a majority of the League of Nine Littérateurs, despite a prolonged presence among them. Even with his solitude, he has a vested interest in Yi Sang, leading to him personally taking the responsibility of his fellow so that the latter may focus on research for N Corp. Upon Yi Sang's departure, Gubo remained composed and cold toward the man, though later reveals a more desperate side, frequently speaking down to his former compatriot and showing interest in his creations, going as far as attempting to reconvince Yi Sang to return to his side as a scientist.
Once elevated to a higher post among the New League of Nine Littérateurs, he carries little care for those beneath his post, shown most prominently in his utilization of E.G.O Gear that benefits from him slaying them. Even still, he is interested in the happenings of Yi Sang, despite their opposing factions.
## Story
### *Leviathan*
Gubo participated as a bidder to the Ring Finger’s auction to retrieve fellow League of Nine Littérateurs researcher, Aseah, who was kidnapped by the Syndicate. Maestro Jumsoon picked up on his intentions, but ordered his men to simply observe him, deeming Gubo to not be a threat yet.
Determined to gain access to the Ring’s corridor by becoming the highest bidder to their auction, Gubo offered an absurd 10 billion Ahn when betting on Jumsoon’s treasured ‘Red Gaze’. However, Vergilius snubbed Gubo’s bid by personally offering Jumsoon to complete the unfinished painting. Despite Gubo’s protests, Jumsoon declared Vergilius’ bid successful and grants him and Garnet access to the Ring’s corridor.
Gubo covered in blood.
His initial plan defunct, Gubo infiltrated the corridor anyway and appeared to have killed several Ring members in his way along, with the other guests in the gallery, losing all of his men in the charge. Hiding behind a hall in the corridor, Vergilius noticed Gubo’s presence and is forced to reveal himself while wearing his coat stained with blood. Gubo went with the claim that he had bathed himself in the blood of the other gallery auctioneers and Ring Fingers he killed in order to lower Vergilius’ guard by feigning injury. But according to Vergilius’ observations, Gubo appeared to have actually sacrificed the men that accompanied him as a merciless price to gain access to the Ring’s corridor.
Gubo attempted to convince Vergilius to accompany him in exchange for whatever the price he wants. The Red Gaze, wise to Gubo's intentions, presented Gubo the ultimatum to provide all the information he has on the Ring’s refraction technology in exchange for Aseah’s rescue. Gubo, unwilling to pay that high price and noting that Virgilius was unarmed, attacked him with his karambit, but is still no match for the Color. Bested in both strength and intel with his life on the line, Gubo reluctantly accepted Vergilus’ terms. The two then made their way to the end of the corridor and into the Ring’s laboratory, encountering the red cocoon Garnet became after being refracted by the glass window.
Gubo then regrouped with Aseah as Vergilius was busy with dispatching the Peccatulum created from the Ring’s experiments. After Aseah complied with Jumsoon’s order of subjecting both he and Vergilius to the refraction technology, the N Corp. scientists observed the two’s fight. When Vergilius awakened to his E.G.O and the Abnormality, Schadenfreude, had burst from Jumsoon’s body, killing him, Gubo and Aseah were satisfied with the results of the glass window and left through a corridor opened by the latter.
### *Limbus Company*
#### Canto I: The Outcast
Jia Huan, Hermann, and Gubo cornering the Sinners.
Gubo makes his debut in *Limbus Company* at the end of Canto I: The Outcast, appearing with N Corp. director Hermann and Jia Huan when confronting the Sinners in Branch D-02. After dispatching the Golden Apple and retrieving the Golden Bough from Yuri's head, Yi Sang lamented his former collegue’s choices as Gubo confirmed to him that “only a few remain now”. The N Corp. team confiscated the Golden Bough from the Sinners as Jia Huan used his abilities to knock all of them unconscious with a clap of his hands.
#### Canto IV: The Unchanging
It is revealed that Gubo was part of the original League of Nine, although it was more as if he were a mere bystander. He didn't get involved much in its affairs until the League finally met its demise; an opportunity he used to get a fragile Yi Sang and lead him to the so-called "White Room", claiming that he should not give up on his life and be taken away by T Corp. There, he was supervised by both him and Hermann, in an attempt to get him to assist them with the Mirror Technology he had created.
Gubo, at that time, was giving Yi Sang pills that forced him to be oblivious to his surroundings and lethargic. He tried persistently to have him abide by Hermann's will, attempting to convince him that this new League that they are forming will be better than the previous one. Once Yi Sang realized the opportunity to escape had long been there, Gubo did nothing to stop him. However, he instead asked him if that was his final decision, claiming that his life from now on would be hopeless, with Yi Sang having nowhere to go. He gave him a notice saying that he will be there at midnight if Yi Sang finally decides against it, but no more effort is shown with preventing him from leaving.
#### Canto VIII: The Surrendered Witnessing
During the Family Hierarch Evaluations, Gubo accompanied Jia Huan to the first trial, wearing E.G.O gear from Der Fluchschütze and carrying the ovate form of an unknown Abnormality. After Jia Huan presented the Abnormality and passed the trial, the two approached the Sinners. While Jia Huan confronted Hong Lu, Gubo greeted Yi Sang, receiving a cold welcome. The two soon departed afterwards.
During round two, Gubo, using his newfound rifle, attempted to shoot at Hong Lu, which Lin Daiyu blocked. Smugly apologizing for the missed shot, he warned the group of the dangers of choosing one's fellows unwisely, leading to a short dialogue in which he and Yi Sang exchanged brief, spiteful words. Disregarding Yi Sang as now "too slick", he was ordered by Jia Huan to stand down. He followed Jia Huan for the remainder of the round and left alongside him when Jia Huan chose to forfeit.
Gubo next appeared alongside Jia Huan at the doors of the Tiekan Temple, where he once again mocked and was rebuffed by Yi Sang. He and Jia Huan forced the Sinners into a temporary truce so they could gain entry. Once inside, they fought the Sinners and lost, but was saved thanks to the defensive shields Gubo installed into their E.G.O gear. After the Sinners' battle with Jia Mu, he used his E.G.O gear's abilities to find and collect the Xianhuang Anamnaworm, which had been the objective behind Jia Huan's participation in the Evaluations, before leaving the Temple with Jia Huan.

## Trivia
Gubo takes his name from the pen name of author Park Taewon. Kubo**, alternately romanized as **Gubo**, was, together with Yi Sang, a member of the literary group, "The Circle of Nine", or *the* *Guinhoe*, which the League of Nine is directly based on.
Park Taewon's novel, *A Day in the Life of Kubo the Novelist* (*Hangul*: 소설가 구보씨의 일일, *Hanja*: 小說家 仇甫氏의 一日) was illustrated by fellow poet Kim Hae-Gyeong, whom Yi Sang is named after, under his alias Ha Yung.
During Yi Sang's time in N Corp., Gubo played a similar role to Yeonsim, the wife of 'I' in The Wings. 'I', the protagonist of the novel, was fed Adalin, a sedative, by Yeonsim, who had told him that it was Aspirin for a cold.
Gubo's knife is a karambit, a curved knife originating from West Sumatra in Indonesia. According to local folklore, the shape of the knife was inspired by the claws of a tiger.
This draws similarities with Yi Sang's knife, a saingeom, which according to Korean folklore was forged during the Year, Month, Day, and Hour of the Tiger.
