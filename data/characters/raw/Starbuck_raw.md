**Starbuck** (Hangul: 스타벅, *Seu-ta-beog*) is the First Mate of The Pequod. Pulled into following Ahab's madness, he would become an enforcer of the town inside the Pallid Whale. He is a supporting character in Canto V: The Evil Defining.

## Appearance
Starbuck appears as a burly man with dark skin and long, black, hair that messily reaches his chin. He has a noticeable scar across his nose, as well as dark stubble on his chin and thick eyebrows. He wears a green button-up shirt with several tears and stitches and rolled up to reveal his forearms, with dark suspenders connecting to his large, tan, wading pants. He wears large, dark, rubber boots over his feet. He is also shown to carry a massive, barbed, harpoon.
In the past during his time on the Pequod, he wore his hair up in a pompadour. In Pequod Town, he is affected by the Pallid Whale's pallidifaction, and has trouble speaking.
## Personality
Starbuck is generally a cheery man, immediately offering the Sinners a hand during their arrival in the Pallid Whale. Despite his kind nature, he is greatly devoted to Ahab as her first mate, so much so that he defended her immediately after Ishmael's aggressive nature being directed towards his captain. He seemingly struggles to come to terms with the deaths of his crewmates, something highlighted by The Pequod First Mate Yi Sang Identity.
## Story
### Prior to Limbus Company*
Prior to the events of Canto V, Starbuck was a devoted member of the Pequod, focused on the hunt of the Pallid Whale. He was close to the crew, and is showcased to be greatly caring and honest to the crew, between playfully teasing Pip as well as comforting Ishmael before the hunt of the Pallid Whale was assumed to be concluded. Like the rest of the crew, he looked up to Ahab as a great leader during this time, even though he also appealed to the others that disagreed with the captain.
### Canto V: The Evil Defining
He was first met when the Sinners arrived inside the Pallid Whale. Soon after, a great amount of acid began to flood the portions where the Sinners were, before Starbuck and members of Pequod Town arrived to save the Sinners and throwing a rope down to the group. As the Sinners closely followed, they arrive to the town led by Starbuck, before Ishmael made an attempt at Ahab's life, spurring the members of the town to fight back. Starbuck made it clear following this confrontation, that they will not allow their captain to be attacked again. Starbuck accompanied Ahab's crew as they traveled to kill the Pallid Whale's heart. He stood by, and possibly even participated, during the slaughter of many of the villagers. Once arrived at the heart, Starbuck battled the Sinners side-by-side with Queequeg and Ahab, only to later be consumed by Ahab's E.G.O, Gasharpoon. He would be turned into fuel and expended in the battle, perishing.

## Trivia
Starbuck shares his namesake with the character of Herman Melville's Moby-Dick, sharing his role as first mate, as well as outwardly showing want to return home.
In chapter 123 of the novel, Starbuck goes down to Ahab's cabin to speak to him, but stops upon seeing a loaded musket, and debates taking it and shooting the Captain, to put an end to the madness of the hunt for Moby-Dick and finally end the voyage, although in the end, he thinks better of it. The scene is referenced in *Limbus Company* by the scene in which Ishmael walks into Ahab's cabin, to find her sleeping with her eyes open, entranced in the thought of killing the Pallid Whale.
