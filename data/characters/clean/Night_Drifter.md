## Night Drifter

### Appearance
The Night Drifter is an elderly man with long gray hair tied behind his head, and a similarly gray beard on his chin, curling at the end. He wears a dingy straw hat with two hanging straps, as well as a large green scarf around his neck. Beneath this scarf, he wears a brown-and-gray hanfu, tied at the waist by a dark sash and ropes. His feet are wrapped in gray cloth, connected to similarly gray soles. He is seen wielding a wooden staff with a single leaf at its top, as well as a large orange drinking gourd with a small yellow sash tied at its middle.

### Personality
The Night Drifter is a highly capable man, reacting laxly while carrying out his assassin work, confident he will succeed. He has a penchant for drinking, which allows for him to better handle his opponents. Like most of those working in line of the familial battles within Daguanyuan, he has little moral qualms with attacking unwilling combatants when ordered to.
He reveals a more moral side during the events of Intervallo VI, having once been a father so wrought by the grief of losing his daughter that he chose to abandon his entire life of notoriety. He views Jia Xichun with a level of grief, saddened in being tasked with killing one of his two final disciples.

### Story
**Prior to Limbus Company***

Once a formidable blade fiend recognized for his skills, the Night Drifter was given the title of one of the Ten Blades of the East. With this immense title and skill, he was feared for his work among Syndicates. However, he would have to bury his daughter who passed during one of Hongyuan's restructuring days, which completely altered the man's view of life.
Wrought with the grief of this event, he chose to forever abandon the use of his blades and instead live as a drunken stranger who abandoned both his name and life.

**Canto VIII: The Surrendered Witnessing**

Following the first round of the Family Hierarch Evaluations, Hong Lu and the Sinners returned to Baoyu's residence. Here, the Night Drifter, alongside a gang of podao-wielding assassins, broke into the residency through the roof. While the Sinners were capable of fending off several of the assassins, the Night Drifter joined the scuffle. Despite Outis attempting to gleam information from the man, he simply states that he has no name yet is known by others as the Night Drifter. Easily dispatching several of the Sinners, the Night Drifter crushes the brain of Hong Lu, lamenting that the victim may have been as skilled as himself, had Hong Lu been trained under his tutelage. Believing his job to be done, the Night Drifter, alongside the remaining assassins, then disappeared into the darkness.
He is later mentioned to have been among the assassins hired by the mother of Xue Baochai, looking to eliminate Hong Lu.

**Intervallo VI: Spring Cultivation**

Amidst the training of Jia Xichun and Sinclair, the Night Drifter was covertly hired by Jia Yuanchun who looked to assassinate the rising Hierarch. However, caught between emotions, Night Drifter instead chose to train the two; even if he were to attempt an assassination, he chose to sell the wolfsbane he was given to buy several expensive wines. During the month of training, Night Drifter diligently trained his disciples.
At the conclusion of the tests, Jia Xichun was kidnapped per the order of Jia Yuanchun, who aimed to kill the Hierarch. The Night Drifter remained responsible for the killing, being her executioner that challenged her to a final duel. This duel was quickly interrupted by both the Sinners arriving and the Heishou Pack's Si branch. Annoyed by the interference, the Night Drifter was incidentally stuck by one of the Si branch members that aimed to kill Xichun, poisoning him. Despite this, he continued the final battle, proclaiming that it would be bad news for Hongyuan if she allowed him to live. In their final battle, he was struck down and killed by Jia Xichun, marking his final lesson to his disciples. Depending if Jia Xichun's level is raised high enough, he either succumbs to the poison first, or is struck down by the Hierarch's skills.
His true identity as an assassin of Jia Yuanchun was ultimately covered up, with the man ending up buried in an unmarked grave and being swept under the rug as a random deranged attacker.

### Trivia
The Night Drifter's fighting style emulates the Chinese martial art of the Drunken Fist style.
Drunken Fist's first written reference is believed to have been in Water Margin, one of the four most prominent pieces of classic Chinese novels. In *Water Margin*, the Drunken Fist style is used by Wu Song, one of the 108 Heroes and the Tianshang Star.
The Night Drifter teaches Xichun and Sinclair the concept of Taoism, a Wu wei.
The Night Drifter's teaching of Emptying sound(无音), Emptying mind(无念), Emptying form(无形) from Intervallo 8.5-4 is a reference to the concept of Taoism. This is a form of "letting go of egoistic concerns" or "Emptying the mind".
