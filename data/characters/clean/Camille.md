## Camille

### Appearance
Camille is a tall man with pale skin and pale blue eyes. He dons the uniform of Cinq Association's Western Branch, consisting of a dark blue shirt with a white cravat, dark blue pants, and knee-high black boots. He wears a white cavalier hat, a white cape lined with white feathers and decorated with gold embroidery, and black gloves similarly lined with feathers. He has wavy, shoulder-length blue hair with light blue highlights, and he wears two star-shaped hairclips.
Following his Corrosion at the hands of E.G.O gear, his clothes are bloodied and pierced by several blooming roses that wrap along his body, blossoming as it feeds on blood. Most notably, the largest rose blooms from the top of his head.

### Personality
Camille is a haughty, attention seeking Fixer who only cares about his popularity. He constantly talks to his stream Chat, showing off anything interesting in his surroundings and incessantly asking for popularity votes on Fixer related polls. He generally disregards people around him, including other Fixers assigned to his mission, the exception being to make fun of them on his stream. He shows a lack of respect for his companion Paula, calling her his ”sidekick” despite their equal rank. Despite his carefree and charming persona, he gets annoyed when Don Quixote’s talk of saving civilians calls into question his own ethics.

### Story
Camille first appeared when the Sinners arrived at the Fixer conference in the District 16 Backstreets, being one of the numerous parties assigned to the mission along with his "sidekick" Paula. After the details of La Manchaland were explained, the pair elaborate that they are on a recovery contract for a specific "guest" at the park, and are aiming to minimize their time there. To Don Quixote’s disapproval, Paula revealed that there are no plans to rescue every stranded person in La Manchaland, to which Don Quixote reacted in confusion, wondering who will save the rest of the visitors. Unfazed, Camille mocked her for this delusion which sparks an argument between the two, boiling over into a duel. Shortly after winning the duel, Camille and the rest of the Fixers at the conference move on to entering La Manchaland.
Discovered further into the Lobotomy Corporation branch within La Manchaland, it was found that the two Corroded while donning E.G.O gear extracted from the Four-hundred Roses Abnormality. Meursault notes the likelihood the two used the gear in a final gambit of survival that ultimately did not succeed. Now mindless due to the Corrosion, the two battled the Sinners and were ultimately put down in the battle.

### Trivia
Camille was listed as one of the "Top Ten Up-and-coming New Generation Fixers" in the October issue of Fixers Monthly*, and mentioned to be a frequent feature on the magazine.
Camille was stated to be in the "Fixers I want to have dinner with" contest, his position being #7 place.
