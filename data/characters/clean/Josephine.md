## Josephine

### Appearance
Josephine has the appearance of an old woman with long, knotted black-and-graying hair pushed out of her face. She wears a monocle over her right eye, and dons a brown suit customary of Wuthering Heights Butlers, with a ribbon around her neck and the cameo of a woman's face (presumably Mrs. Earnshaw) on her left lapel. On her left arm, she carries a white cloth.

### Personality
Josephine is an erratic woman who frequently falls into fits of what seems to be madness, ranting about the late mistress of the house as if she were still present. Despite this, she is fiercely loyal and looks down upon those around her, talking down to Nelly and frequently outwardly disagreeing with the current state of the manor, seeing it as a former shadow of itself.

### Story
**Prior to Limbus Company***

At some point in her life, Josephine was hired by Mrs. Earnshaw to work as her primary Butler. Following the mistress' death, she remained in service to the estate, though consistently believed herself to be hearing the voice of the late lady.
Through Catherine's journal, it would be made known that Josephine knew of Wuthering Heights being built upon an ancient river--something that the contractors believed should not have been done. Because of this, both Cathy and Josephine believed to see the deceased lady's ghost wandering the halls.

**Canto VI: The Heartbreaking**

Josephine was present for the reading of Catherine's will, lamenting the state of the manor to the late mistress, Catherine's mother. Nelly told the Sinners that she is not worth paying any mind. After a short refutation from the Chief Butler, Josephine idly stood by during the will reading.
Following the first strike of lightning, she was transported along with several butlers to a corridor within the manor, the same that Hindley, Dead Rabbits Syndicate members, and the Sinners were sent to. Blaming the situation on Hindley, she began to plan his death, only interrupted by the Sinners fighting off her and her Butlers. Following their defeat, the Butlers then retreated deeper into the manor.
Later, during the confrontation with Erlking Heathcliff in the heart of the manor's basement, Josephine arrived in the midst of the situation, panicking. During her manic stressing, she was impaled by Erlking Heathcliff, who brought her into his Wild Hunt.

### Trivia
Josephine is based on Joseph from *Wuthering Heights*.
Similarly to Josephine, Joseph is a insufferably zealous man who has served Wuthering Heights his entire life, remaining tied to the house even after Hindley Earnshaw's death.
Josephine's dialogue uses the older English pronoun "ye" and truncates "of" as "o'"; this is presumably intended to reflect Joseph's Yorkshire dialect in *Wuthering Heights*.
