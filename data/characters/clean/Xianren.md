## Xianren

### Overview
The venerated elders of Hongyuan, each Xianren was once a well-known Hierarch of the District. In the process of succession, each retiring elder passes the inherited Xianhuang Anamnaworm to the next elder, before being sealed within Tiekan Temple alongside the rest of the group, where they live with their own, unique, forms of immortality. This allows all Executive Directors to supervise the Wing even long after their supposed deaths, but deteriorates their sanity entirely as a side effect of inheriting the Anamnaworm and no longer being able to physically interact with the outside world.
Located deep within Daguanyuan, the passageways to Tiekan Temple are winding and exceptionally exhausting, with few members even knowing of the path to it. At the gates of the temple underground, just beyond a decorated bridge over a small lake, is a massive jade slot in the center, a gateway only opened either when the Xianren themselves invite somebody, or by Hong Lu bringing his eye to the doorway.
The room is dark, with many ornate pillars and seatings around the center of the room, where a giant screen rests on the ground, giving a live feed of all that Hong Lu witnesses via his left eye. After the events of Canto VIII: The Surrendered Witnessing, the room becomes flooded and blue, disconnected from the outside world due to the flooding.
Each elder features a complete unique design from one another, embodying their use of different methods of immortality. In all cases, this involves them becoming grotesquely deformed and their minds warped into madness.

### Members
Three Xianren (Elder in a Jar, Elder in Monk Robes, and Elder in a Machine) were named in The Lord of Hongyuan Hong Lu's Uptie Story. As the other Xianren do not feature unique names in-game or in files, they are given names which are unofficial designations used for the sake of simplicity.*

One of the many Xianren, this elder is a figure composed of congealed blood, sitting on a small branch with his legs in a lotus position with his wrists crossed. Her bottom half is obscured in a bloodied robe, and she sits in a rusty wheelchair, featuring an attachment that carries twelve bags of blood, all of which are attached to various points across her body.

### Story
**Prior to *Limbus Company***

The first elder of Daguanyuan became an Anamnaworm, one which would be passed down across each following Hierarch in a mean to accrue exponential knowledge across each succeeding Hierarch. One of these elders was recognized by Jia Xichun as her great-great-grandfather, who snuck her snacks and was known for his fondness for walks.
As the Xianren's lifespans were so long, they found that the true danger of immortality was boredom. As a way to combat this, on Jia Baoyu's birth, Jia Mu implanted a jade into his left eye, making him a treasured piece of Hongyuan's culture. In reality, this jade allowed for everything that Jia Baoyu experienced to be sent to a screen in the Tiekan Temple, to provide the Xianren with unique entertainment from within their chambers.
In Jia Baoyu's youth, he would be repeatedly summoned to the Tiekan Temple, where they would meet with him as a way to treasure him for his status as the "jade of Hongyuan", something which Jia Baoyu feared at first, yet grew apathetic and desensitized to. On some occasions, Jia Baoyu witnessed a 'molting' process, where the elders fed on unknowing servants, splitting their corpses between each member as means to remain living.
One event of which the Xianren were particularly fond of was the massacre of the Kong family, to which they grew especially fond of Jia Mu for having caused.
In a bid to continue creating new entertainment for the elders, Jia Mu had Jia Baoyu leave Daguanyuan, coinciding with when the man was contracted by Faust for Limbus Company. This would cause the Xianren to witness the events of every single Canto, growing fond of the Sinners from a detached point of interest.

**Canto VIII: The Surrendered Witnessing**

Once Jia Xichun had managed to secure the role of H Corp. Hierarch, she was required to be annointed officially by meeting with the Xianren in Tiekan Temple, accepting the Anamnaworm, and then assuming the throne in Daguanyuan. The Sinners arrived to the temple while this was in the process of unfolding, where Jia Xichun grew terrified of the Anamnaworm, worried to become another immortality-obsessed Xianren who witnesses brutality through Hong Lu's jade. While the Xianren were initially joyful at meeting the Sinners in the flesh, they quickly grew annoyed at the insubordination against the passage of the Anamnaworm. Jia Mu battled the Sinners, utilizing the forbidden Heixian Bolus to inherit the attributes of every Heishou Pack Branch. During this battle, she consumed several of the elders in a desperate plea to regain her footing against the Sinners, and harness the consumed elder's immortality.
At the climax of this battle, Hong Lu utilizes an empowered Land of Illusion to release a flood of clear water, knocking the Golden Bough embedded into Jia Mu's chest free and flooding the entire temple. As the Xianren are collectively knocked away from their seats, Jia Huan angrily approached the screen in the center of the building and shattered it. While several of the elders attempted to crawl out of the gateway of the temple, they were unable to, and were sealed within as Hong Lu wished them goodbye, permanently sealing them inside.
While the Xianren each utilize means of flawed immortality, only their natural lifespans are extended, and their biological processes are not stopped. Without their sustenance, influence, or entertainment, Meursault hypothesizes they will very slowly pass away while locked within the chamber.

### Trivia
In Taoist culture, Xian is a term used to classify any form of mythological creature that has achieved immortality. Expanding on this, the specific term of Xianren means "immortal person."
Several times, the Xianren are alternatively referred to as the Enlightened Ones in Canto VIII.
While Taoist Xian are generally depicted as benevolent and wise men unconcerned by worldly ideas, the Xianren in *Limbus Company* represent the opposite of this idea.
After Hong Lu's final attack against Jia Mu, the seating of the temple seen in the Story Background is visibly cleared of every Xianren.
Lady Wang mentions that Hong Lu's birthname, Jia Baoyu, was bestowed upon him by the Xianren.
